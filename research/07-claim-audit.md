# Which claims need correction or caution?

## Overall finding

The original report's major arithmetic is generally sound, and its infrasound discussion is more cautious than many advocacy summaries. Its central weakness is that generalized risk research is wrapped around an unverified and largely undisclosed local project.

## Correct before public distribution

### Project description lacks a source

The 125-acre location and project description need an application, parcel record, agenda packet, or attributed source. Until then, label them unverified.

### Agenda search was incomplete

The report says the online archive exposed only a January 13 agenda. The cited legacy archive does, but the county also operates a separate Agenda Center and links newer agendas from its commissioners page. Say the legacy archive was incomplete, not that the entire website contained only one 2026 agenda.

### Meeting date is inferred

The commissioners page gives a recurring third-Tuesday, 6 p.m. schedule. July 21 follows from that rule, but the cited page does not itself display a July 21 agenda. Say the schedule “indicates” that date and confirm special or rescheduled meetings separately.

### Irish source title is wrong

The document linked in footnote 33 is a September 2024 Operational Report for South Dublin Routing 4 No. 2 Limited. The technical description drawn from it is supported.

## Add context

### Historical groundwater finding

The old USGS report documented a small cone of depression near Americus, but also concluded that broader groundwater resources were not significantly impaired by 1990 use. Both findings belong together.

### County electricity estimate

The 406,990 MWh figure is from FindEnergy, not a utility or EIA county total. The website warns that its compilation may be incomplete or inconsistent. Keep it as a provisional planning estimate.

### Virginia complaint measurements

JLARC reported complaint-associated readings around 40–59 dBA, but noted that these were total sound measurements and were not representative of all data centers. The 15 problematic sites were identified from resident groups and government records, not a controlled prevalence study.

### Americus water-quality PDF

The city-hosted 2024 report supports the statement that water comes from groundwater wells. Other portions contain an out-of-state telephone number and inconsistent lead figures, indicating a template or editing problem. Cite it only for claims clearly present and internally coherent.

## Keep labeled as scenarios

- Evaporative water use at 25–200 MW
- Diesel and gas use at 100 MW continuous generation
- Passenger-car CO₂ equivalents
- Facility electricity compared with county consumption
- Universal household solar and battery fleet totals

None of those figures predicts the Americus proposal until its load, cooling design, equipment, and operating schedule are disclosed.

## Policy proposal—not established cost

The approximately $1.5 billion household-energy trust target is an advocacy design. The cited NREL system is much smaller than the proposed battery package and does not validate the per-home allowance. It also requires Georgia land-use, utility, tax, public-finance, and constitutional review.
