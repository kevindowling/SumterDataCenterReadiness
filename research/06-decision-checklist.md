# What should officials require before a vote?

## The one-page checklist

### Project definition

- [ ] Named applicant, owner, operator, tenant, and responsible parent company
- [ ] Full-buildout buildings, phases, schedule, IT MW, meter MW, and generator MW
- [ ] Conditions binding tenants, successors, and assigns
- [ ] Public application, staff report, ordinance, and agreements with review time

### Water

- [ ] Cooling schematic naming every dry, wet, adiabatic, and hybrid component
- [ ] Average-day, peak-day, maximum-month, and annual water balance
- [ ] Source identified; no later source change without review
- [ ] City capacity certification covering permit, wells, storage, pressure, drought, and fire flow
- [ ] Blowdown flow, chemistry, treatment, and discharge destination
- [ ] Baseline and continuing nearby-well monitoring

### Sound

- [ ] Independent baseline and full-buildout acoustic study
- [ ] A-, C-, and Z-weighting plus one-third-octave results
- [ ] Nighttime, tonal, modulation, and indoor protections
- [ ] No HVAC, transformer, substation, or generator exemption
- [ ] Peak-load commissioning test and complaint protocol

### Air and generation

- [ ] Every engine and turbine identified by exact model
- [ ] Hours separated by testing, emergency, demand response, and grid delay
- [ ] Narrow definition of emergency
- [ ] Cumulative air permit and conservative dispersion model
- [ ] Controls, fuel standards, hour meters, and public operating logs
- [ ] No routine bridge generation without separate power-plant review

### Electricity

- [ ] Firm will-serve letter with dates and capacity by phase
- [ ] Project-funded dedicated and attributable upgrades
- [ ] Long-term minimum payments and financial backstops
- [ ] Ride-through, ramp, protection, harmonics, and stability studies
- [ ] Curtailment plan that protects firm residential and essential loads

### Site and lifecycle

- [ ] Stormwater, downstream flooding, wetlands, species, and cultural review
- [ ] Fire, battery, transformer, fuel, gas, and contaminated-water plans
- [ ] Construction hours, routes, dust, vibration, and road-damage controls
- [ ] Tax, job, school, emergency-service, and net-fiscal analysis
- [ ] Decommissioning plan and periodically updated financial assurance

## Every condition needs six parts

1. A numerical or objectively testable limit
2. A measurement method
3. A reporting interval
4. Public access to results
5. A defined violation and cure period
6. A meaningful enforcement consequence

Promises such as “sustainable,” “closed loop,” “minimal impact,” or “industry standard” are not enforceable unless translated into those parts.

## If the packet is still incomplete

> Table any final action that would create or vest development rights. Publish the complete full-buildout application, draft rules, utility commitments, and independent impact analysis, then schedule a noticed hearing after meaningful public review.
