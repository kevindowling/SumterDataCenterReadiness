# Sumter County Data Center Community Research Report

## Start here

This is the community research report for the proposed Sumter County data center. It is organized as short, meeting-ready guides so residents can find evidence, frame questions, and ask commissioners for enforceable answers.

The most important fact is also the largest information gap: no complete public project application or engineering package was located. The large planning numbers in this report are therefore **scenarios**, not forecasts for this site.

## July 21 public meeting

The Sumter County calendar lists a **Regular Board Meeting on Tuesday, July 21, 2026 at 6:00 p.m.**, at **500 West Lamar Street, Americus, Georgia 31709**. The meeting is open to the public.

This report is intended to help residents bring focused questions about water, sound, air pollution, electricity, public costs, and enforceable conditions. The county calendar confirms the meeting; residents should still check the posted agenda and any last-minute county notices before attending.

## What is known

- Americus municipal water comes from groundwater wells.
- The city holds groundwater-withdrawal permit 129-0001.
- The permit lists 3.75 million gallons per day (MGD) as the annual-average limit and 4.2 MGD as the monthly-average limit.
- The Development Authority advertises approximately 2.5 MGD of average city water consumption and a 7 MGD water-plant capacity.
- A 125-acre tract has been described as the proposed location, but the underlying public application should be obtained and cited.

## What is not known publicly

- The developer, operator, and tenant
- Full-buildout IT load and total electrical demand
- Final cooling and heat-rejection design
- Average and peak water demand
- Generator count, models, fuel, and allowed operating hours
- Utility service commitments and upgrade costs
- Full project phasing, buildings, and schedule

## How to read the labels

- **Verified fact** — supported by a named source.
- **Scenario** — arithmetic showing scale, not a prediction for Americus.
- **Unknown** — project-specific information that was not located publicly.
- **Recommendation** — a proposed safeguard or policy choice, not an existing legal requirement.
- **Caution** — a claim that is incomplete, weakly sourced, or easy to misstate.

## What this report promises

This report will distinguish sourced facts from scenarios, unknowns, and recommendations. Where a local project fact has not been documented, it says so. The verification note identifies the records and answers still needed before commissioners act.
